Recommendation Letter Policy

I am happy to write letters of recommendation for students who meet the following criteria:

  • You must have completed (or nearly completed) at least one class with me or worked with me on independent research.
  • I need at least three weeks (preferably four) advance notice. I am unable to write good letters on short notice.
  • I prefer to meet to discuss the opportunity or program you’re applying to so I can understand your motivation, your goals beyond the program, and the aspects you want me to highlight in the letter.
  • When you request a letter, please be prepared to provide me (1) information about the program you’re applying to (website, program description, selection criteria) (2) the submission link or process, and (3) reasonable drafts of your application materials (e.g. statements, CV/resume, writing samples). Please send me finished versions of the materials when they are available.

Please note that my policy is to provide honest recommendations. If you haven’t performed well in my class, you may want to request a letter from a different professor.

Prospective Graduate Students

For students interested in applying to the Sociology Ph.D. program, please know that our department admits students collectively and not for specific faculty members. Contacting me will not improve your chances of admission.

I’d be happy to answer research related questions, but for questions about applications, timeline, or logistics you should contact our Graduate Administrate Assistant.
