Michael Schultz

I’m an assistant professor of sociology at Indiana University Bloomington.

Previously, I was a post-doctoral researcher at Northwestern University in the Science of Networks in Communities (SONIC) research group. I received my Ph.D. in Sociology from the University of California, Berkeley in August 2016.

I’m a computational social scientist who studies organizations, communication, and quantitative methodologies. I’m particularly interested in how the structure of communication affects organizational and economic outcomes.

My research has looked at the the effects of communication structures within and across organizations, including the Federal Reserve, NASA, and international diplomacy. My methodological research seeks to harmonize theoretical and statistical considerations in quantitative research, particularly with respect to model selection and abductive research designs.
