I am an Assistant Professory of Sociology at Indiana University Bloomington. Previously, I was a Post-Doctoral Researcher at Northwestern University in the Science of Networks in Communities (SONIC) research group. I received my Ph.D. in Sociology from the University of California, Berkeley in August 2016. I work at the intersection of computational social science, economic sociology and social psychology. My research examines the structure of social interactions and social relations. It seeks to understand (1) how social interactions are conditioned by their context and (2) how patterns of social action combine to create macrosocial structure. My research has explored the dynamics of interactions within organizations, including the Federal Reserve and NASA, and analyzed the diverse structures created by confidant relationships, international diplomatic missions, and lifestyle preferences. I like mixed methods approaches to research. I primarily use computational methods (e.g. nonparametric statistics, network analysis, text analysis, and information theory), but I also happily employ qualitative methods when the research calls for it.
