I am a Post-Doctoral Researcher at Northwestern University in the Science of Networks in Communities (SONIC) research group. I received my Ph.D. in Sociology from the University of California, Berkeley in August 2016. I work at the intersection of economic sociology, social psychology, and computational social science. My research examines the structure of social interactions and social relations. It seeks to understand (1) how social interactions are conditioned by their context and (2) how patterns of social action combine to create macrosocial structure. My research has explored the dynamics of interactions within organizations, including the Federal Reserve and NASA, and analyzed the diverse structures created by confidant relationships, diplomatic missions, and lifestyle preferences. I am a supporter of a mixed methods approaches. My research primarily uses computational methods (e.g. nonparametric statistics, network analysis, text analysis, and information theory), but I also happily employ qualitative research methods when appropriate.
